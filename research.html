Research

My research focuses on the impacts of land use, urbanization, and climate change on migratory birds, cryptic wetland birds, and woodpeckers.
Browse below for project details.


Competition under climate change

We are interested in understanding how climate change influences competition and aggression between species. How do rising temperatures influence endotherm behaviors, and which species are most vulnerable to temperature-induced behavioral changes? We are especially interested in studying species operating at or near their thermal range limits.


Using environmental DNA to improve bird detections

Cryptic wetland birds, such as rails and bitterns, are difficult to detect in the field, leaving us with a poor understanding of their distributions and basic life histories. We are developing environmental DNA sampling methods to improve detections of multiple cryptic wetland bird species. Improved detections will help us better understand the timing of breeding and migration for these enigmatic species, and help us understand how they respond to wetland management.


Multi-species occupancy modelling

We are investigating how urbanization and forest structure drive migratory bird diversity and influence stopover habitat use.


Red-headed woodpecker conservation

Red-headed woodpeckers are declining in the northern part of their range. We are investigating how urbanization, habitat type, acorn availability, and age structure affect Red-headed woodpecker territory establishment and reproductive success.
