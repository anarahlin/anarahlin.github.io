Research

My research focuses on the impacts of land use, urbanization, and climate change on migratory birds, cryptic wetland birds, and woodpeckers.
Browse below for project details.

Climate change and

aggression

We are interested in understanding how climate change influences competition and aggression between species, and how rising temperatures influence endotherm behaviors, especially for species operating at the edges of their thermal ranges.

Improving wetland bird

detections with

environmental DNA

Cryptic wetland birds, such as rails and bitterns, are difficult to detect in the field, leaving us with a poor understanding of their distributions and basic life history information. We are developing environmental DNA sampling methods to improve detections of multiple cryptic wetland bird species. Improved detections will help us better understand the timing of breeding and migration, and help us understand how species respond to wetland management.

Red-headed woodpecker

conservation

Red-headed woodpeckers are declining in the northern part of their range. We are investigating how urbanization, habitat type, acorn availability, and age structure affect Red-headed woodpecker territory establishment and reproductive success.
